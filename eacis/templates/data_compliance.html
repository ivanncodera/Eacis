COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK

Data Compliance & Security

Overview of our compliance posture, technical controls, processors, and how we manage personal data in line with applicable law.

E-ACIS Data Compliance Summary

Document date: April 18, 2026

1. Scope and Objectives

This page describes operational and technical measures used to protect personal data, how we vet processors, and our approach to regulatory compliance. It is informational and does not replace our Privacy Policy or Terms.

2. Governance and Roles

We maintain a Data Protection Officer (DPO) and a compliance team responsible for policies, vendor assessments, incident response, staff training, and responding to data subject rights requests.

Contact: dpo@e-acis.ph

3. Technical Controls

  • Encryption: Data in transit is protected via TLS; sensitive persisted data is encrypted or tokenized where applicable.
  • Access Controls: Role-based access and least-privilege principles for system components and admin tooling.
  • Logging & Monitoring: Audit logs are retained for security and compliance review; anomaly detection is employed for fraud prevention.
  • Backups: Regular encrypted backups with tested restore procedures and retention policies.

4. Processors and Vendor Management

We contractually bind processors (payment gateways, shipping partners, cloud infrastructure, analytics) to limit use and ensure security. New vendors undergo security and privacy assessments, and subprocessors are tracked.

5. Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)

DPIAs are performed for higher-risk features (KYC, biometric-like verification, profiling for credit/installment eligibility). Documentation is retained and reviewed as product features evolve.

6. Incident Response & Reporting

We maintain an incident response playbook with roles, containment, notification to regulators where required, and post-incident reviews. Affected users are notified in alignment with applicable laws.

7. PCI and Payment Security

Payments are processed by PCI-compliant providers. Cardholder data is not stored on E-ACIS unless explicitly tokenized via our payment partners; merchants and sellers must follow payment provider terms.

8. Audits, Certifications and Continuous Improvement

We perform periodic security testing and audits. Where feasible, we pursue recognized standards and certifications; security and compliance activities are part of our product lifecycle and release processes.

9. Requests and Record Access

Data subjects may request copies of records or ask for information about processing. Requests should be sent to the DPO or via the Support Center; identity verification is required before fulfilling requests.

10. Limitations and Legal Advice

This page provides an operational overview and should not be construed as legal advice. For binding guidance about compliance obligations in your jurisdiction, consult qualified counsel or the relevant supervisory authority.
